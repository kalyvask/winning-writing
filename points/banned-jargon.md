# Banned jargon

Kill on sight. These words and phrases mark you as a consultant, an AI, or someone with nothing to say.

## The official banned list (Kramon)

`currently` is the class-wide banned word — the entire class pays if anyone uses it. The rest are nearly as bad.

### Silicon Valley / academic jargon

- **synergy / synergize**
- **leverage** (you leverage cars, not opportunities)
- **align**
- **drive** (you drive cars, not strategy)
- **strategize** (toxic — confirms the consultant stereotype)
- **empower**
- **enable**
- **deliverables**
- **utilize** → use **use**
- **incentivize**
- **facilitate**
- **impact / negative impacts / positive impacts** → use **harm** / **benefits**
- **at the intersection of [X, Y, Z]** — the most expensive intersection in Silicon Valley
- **driving innovation**
- **building and scaling**
- **passionate about complex problems**
- **I'm left brain and right brain**

### Grammar errors that signal carelessness

- **irregardless** → **regardless**
- **literally** when used metaphorically
- **ironic** when you mean **coincidental**

### Wordy phrases — cut every time

| ❌ Don't write | ✅ Write instead |
|---|---|
| In the event that | If |
| Concerning the matter of | About |
| I came to the realization that | I realized |
| We are investigating | We're investigating |
| Negative impacts | Harm |
| Positive impacts | Benefits |
| Sorry for the delay | Thanks for your patience |
| I think maybe we should | Let's |
| What works best for you? | A specific proposal |
| In today's fast-paced world | (delete entirely) |
| I hope this email finds you well | (delete entirely) |
| Just wanted to check in | (delete entirely — write a real email or don't write) |
| I think (when you actually know) | (state what you know) |

## Cold-email-specific killers (Konrad)

- **"I hope you are well"** — signals you have nothing interesting to say
- **"My name is [X]"** — it's in the email header
- **"Good morning / afternoon"** — you don't know their time zone
- **"I'd love to pick your brain"** — offer something instead
- **"I'd love to grab coffee"** — be specific to them
- **"Like you, but at a vastly smaller scale"** — self-diminishing; they already know that
- **"If you ever find yourself near Stanford"** — say "I will come to you, anywhere"
- **"Just following up"** — write a new email with new information

## AI tells

ChatGPT-flavored constructions to scrub:

- **"It's not just X — it's Y."** (the most common AI tic)
- **"In today's [adjective] world"**
- **"Delve into"**
- **"Navigate the complexities of"**
- **"Tapestry of"**
- **"Robust solution"**
- **"Cutting-edge"**
- **"Game-changer"**
- Em-dashes used as a tic (one or two per page is fine — twelve is a tell)
- Tricolons everywhere ("X, Y, and Z" three times in three sentences)

## How to use this list

1. Run a find-and-highlight on your draft.
2. For each hit, ask: *what do I actually mean?* The replacement is almost always a shorter, plainer word.
3. If you can't find a replacement, the sentence is probably empty and should be cut entirely.
